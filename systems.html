Assignment


Create Three Information maps:

Table or matrix, e.g., name-value pairs
Create a list—a table with each person in your family history listed as a row. Go back to your great grandparents. (You may use any family tree.) Include columns for last name, first name, date of birth, death, location of main residence, pointer to a portrait. Include another column listing connections.

Sequence or array, e.g., a slideshow or timeline Create a timeline including all the members of your family tree. Include names and indicate the range of time during which they lived.

Tree or hierarchy, e.g., root-node-leaf or parent-child-sibling Create a diagram of your family tree, going back to your great grandparents. Include names, portraits, and dates of birth/death.










Information Structures - Interactive


Mar 12, 2018 · Sohaj Singh Brar

Assignment


Create an interactive information structure, based on your three maps.
Make interactive versions of your list, timeline, and family tree.
Each person is a “node;” each “node” will be a “slide” or “card” or “page,”
and each link will be a “hyperlink.”


Methods used


I purposely used different methods for all three interactive elements for this assignment i.e. D3Js to build interactive family tree, FramerJS for Timeline and JQuery for Table. Using different methods of JS, unlike keynote/AE/principle etc. I learned how I can now apply any of these methods to build interactive systems that can be viewed/interacted anywhere in the world through webpages. Applying different JS techniques helped me learn which one is best suited for what purpose. And based on my expertise and the project type, I can choose the right tool in the future.

Product-Service Ecology — Wellness


Apr 24, 2018 · Sohaj Singh Brar

Hypertension


Imagine sensors that monitor body chemistry painlessly and connect to “the cloud”. Imagine other cloud-connected devices that deliver therapies. How could such technology be integrated into people’s lives to help them and their caregivers better manage chronic conditions? Design an integrated “ecology”.




What is Hypertension


A condition in which the force of the blood against the artery walls is too high. Blood pressure levels fluctuate throughout the day and can easily change as a result of stress, physical activity and even talking.

You can have high blood pressure and experience no obvious symptoms until you experience a stroke or heart attack. In some people, severe high blood pressure can result in nosebleeds, headaches, or dizziness
